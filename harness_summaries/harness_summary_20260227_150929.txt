Title: Building 70 SaaS products to stress-test my AI build harness — 5 down, 65 to go
Body:
I built a deterministic SaaS harness with 53 capabilities (auth, payments, fraud detection, GDPR compliance, the works). Now I need to know if it actually works at scale.
So I'm building 70 different SaaS products through it. Real FastAPI + React + Stripe deployments. If the harness breaks, I fix it. If it holds, I know it's solid.
Here's what the harness logged from builds 1-5:
Product                      | Runs | Passes | Avg Cost | Main Failure Mode
-----------------------------|------|--------|----------|-------------------
Adversarial Ai Validator     |    7 |      2 |    $0.60 | CTRL_C
Wynwood Thoroughbreds        |    5 |      2 |    $0.63 | BUILD_TRUNCATED
Ai Workforce Intelligence    |   11 |      4 |    $1.00 | NON_CONVERGING (15 iters, $5.4001)
Freelance Invoice Tracker    |    2 |      1 |    $1.65 | CTRL_C
Property Manager Maintenance |    1 |      1 |    $0.65 | None

Total: 26 runs, 10 clean builds (38% first-pass success), $29.56 spent
What I learned:
- High failure rates in Adversarial AI Validator (CTRL_C) indicate potential stability issues.
- Ai Workforce Intelligence showed non-convergence at 15 iterations, highlighting scalability limits.
- Average costs varied significantly; Freelance Invoice Tracker peaked at $3.99, suggesting inefficiencies.
- Wynwood Thoroughbreds faced repeated BUILD_TRUNCATED failures, indicating build process fragility.
- CTRL_C failures across multiple products suggest a common underlying issue in execution stability.

Following this for the next 6 months. Will post failures and edge cases as I find them.
